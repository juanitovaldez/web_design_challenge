Do Earthquakes emit measurable gasses?

A few hackish attempts at visualizations.

A Cocktail of Gasses

A selection of promising pollutant classes were tested and sampled for 7 days before and after each earthquake event. The pollutants under study are sulfur dioxide, Nitrous Oxide. For each class a jitter column plot and violin plot were used to visualize the distribution of each stations reading on each day. Then a Kolmogorov-Schmirnov matrix was calculated to test if each pairwise date combo is distributed differently from it's partner. Our results were inconclusive.

Kolmogorov-Schmirnov Matrix

The Kolmogorov-Smirnov two sample test statistic can be defined as follows:

H0: The two samples come from a common distribution.

Ha: The two samples do not come from a common distribution.

The hypothesis regarding the distributional form is rejected if the test statistic, D, is greater than the critical value.

For a 95% critical value (alpha = 0.05) for the K-S two sample test statistic we reject H0

Strip Plot

The strip plot should visually confirum what we find with the two sample KS test. Where the value in the KS matrix is >= 0.05 we may be able to see a difference in the distrubution at the higher end. The density of the points makes this difficult but the effect is observable in a few cases where the alpha is much larger than the threshold for rejection. We have alot of noise at the bottom end of the measurement spectrum. Thinning out our station selection to exclude farther monitoring sights could help clear this issue up.

Violin Plot

The violing plot supports the strip plot by allowing another vantage point on how the distribution changes with time. Once again, we should see subtle variations in the shape of each day, especially so where the KS-alpha is much greater than the threshold.

cO KS Matrix

We can see that two days prior to and after the event may come from a different distribution. This could imply that some physical process is changing the magnitude of the measurement signals.
